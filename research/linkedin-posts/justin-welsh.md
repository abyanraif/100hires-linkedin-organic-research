## Post 2
- Date: 2 months ago 
- URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_most-people-think-theyre-good-communicators-activity-7443262639448797184-p22Z?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAABNnNbsBtSA-me_2hAEexc6JLi2H5oIV0uo 
- Main topic: Audience-first communication and message clarity
- Notes: This post emphasizes that strong communication starts with the audience’s information needs, not the speaker’s perspective. It is relevant to B2B SaaS LinkedIn strategy because effective content, landing pages, and messaging depend on clarity, relevance, and understanding what the reader actually needs to know.
- Post text:
  Most people think they're good communicators. 

  They check in.
  They give you the background. 
  They explain what they're thinking. 
  They "over-communicate" in their eyes.

  But the audience on the other end of the conversation is still missing the most important stuff.

  So, they're stuck sitting around and wondering:

  "What about the ONE thing I actually need to know?"

  The problem is almost never a lack of effort. 

  People work hard on their message, their presentations, and their pitches.

  They come prepared, and they genuinely care.

  But they communicate from their own perspective instead of the perspective of the audience. 

  They share what's interesting to them rather than what's useful to the listener.

  And the gap between those two things is where you lose.

  On a landing page. 
  In a text message. 
  In a boardroom. 
  In a pitch.

  Here's the one question you always need to keep in mind:

  "What specific information does this person need from me right now?"

  Tomorrow's newsletter is about the consequences of forgetting to ask that question and how to make sure you're communicating effectively.
