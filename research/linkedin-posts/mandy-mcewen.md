- Post text: 
  LinkedIn's video reach is pretty weak sauce right now, but it's still driving revenue. 

  So is YouTube and every other social media channel. 

  Remind me again why more B2B companies aren't truly leveraging video? 

  B2C brands have been pumping out legit videos for a hot minute now. 

  Meanwhile, a lot of B2B companies are still treating video like it’s some massive production.

  Lights.
  Scripts.
  Perfect hair.
  A 47-step approval process.

  Completely unnecessary.

  Your buyers are already watching video everywhere else.

  They watch...

  Product demos.
  Founder videos.
  Customer stories.
  Behind-the-scenes content.
  Quick explainers.
  Industry hot takes.

  Then they get to B2B and well, good luck finding a ton of companies capitalizing on video properly. Some are, yes. Most are not.

  If you want your company to use video better, here are 5 places to start: 

  1. Stop overproducing everything.

  A helpful 60-second video beats a polished brand video nobody watches. Or even better, record a long-form podcast episode and chop it up into clips.

  2. Show actual humans.

  Buyers trust people, not logos. Put your sales team, leaders, experts, and customers on camera.

  3. Answer real buyer questions. Turn FAQs, objections, sales calls, and customer conversations into short videos.

  4. Use video in the sales process.

  Send quick LinkedIn videos, follow-up videos, recap videos, and personalized explainers. Stop relying only on emails that land in inbox purgatory.

  5. Repurpose the hell out of it.

  One video can become clips, LinkedIn posts, carousels, emails, sales enablement content, and website content.

  Video is not just for B2C brands selling who are selling way cooler things than software.

  Remember, B2B buyers are still humans that buy all sorts of things.

  They want clarity.
  They want trust.
  They want to see who they’re buying from.

  And video does that faster than almost anything else.
